Meituan released its 2025 annual report yesterday. Full-year revenue came in at RMB 364.9 billion, up 8%, but operating profit swung from RMB 36.8 billion in 2024 to a loss of RMB 17 billion (unadjusted), with the operating margin dropping from 10.9% to -4.7%. Looking at Q4 alone, however, things improved relative to Q3: Q4 operating losses totaled roughly RMB 14.6 billion (Core Local Commerce -10 billion, New Initiatives -4.6 billion), narrowing about 5% from Q3's RMB 15.4 billion (Core Local Commerce -14.1 billion, New Initiatives -1.3 billion). Core Local Commerce losses narrowed 29% quarter-over-quarter.

## How Much Did Each Player Burn in Q4 2025?

Start with the competitors. JD.com, which kicked off the food delivery war, reported full-year losses of RMB 46.6 billion from new businesses (primarily food delivery), with Q4 losses of RMB 14.8 billion — down 6% from Q3's RMB 15.7 billion. JD.com's full-year marketing expenses surged from RMB 48 billion to RMB 84 billion, a 75% year-over-year increase. One year after launching food delivery, the new business segment generated full-year revenue of RMB 49.3 billion, but most of it was consumed by massive subsidies — a steep price to pay.

Alibaba's Q4 instant retail revenue reached RMB 20.8 billion (+56% YoY). Alibaba doesn't separately disclose instant retail losses, but we can back into the number from the China Commerce segment's adjusted EBITA, which fell 43% year-over-year to RMB 34.6 billion — a decline of roughly RMB 26.5 billion, the vast majority driven by instant retail subsidies. Broker estimates put the segment's quarterly loss at RMB 20–25 billion, with per-order losses declining from about RMB 5 in the prior quarter to roughly RMB 3.5. On the March 19 earnings call, Jiang Fan stated explicitly that instant retail is not expected to break even until fiscal year 2029 — in other words, profitability is off the table for the next three years.

Now for Meituan: Q4 Core Local Commerce posted an operating loss of RMB 10 billion, narrowing 29% from Q3's RMB 14.1 billion. Core Local Commerce includes both food delivery and in-store services, so there's no standalone food delivery P&L. But based on broker estimates, the food delivery segment's Q4 loss was in the range of RMB 10–13 billion, with a per-order loss of roughly RMB 2.

Combined Q4 burn across all three: Meituan ~RMB 14.6 billion + JD.com RMB 14.8 billion + Alibaba ~RMB 25 billion ≈ **RMB 54.4 billion** — in a single quarter.

One detail speaks volumes: Meituan's per-order loss was roughly RMB 2, compared to Alibaba's roughly RMB 3.5. The efficiency gap in delivery networks becomes even more visible when everyone is bleeding cash.

## Resilience Beyond the Headlines

Despite fierce competition and another quarter of heavy losses, Meituan's business showed several bright spots:

**Flash Sale remains on a fast growth track.** Q4 Flash Sale GTV and order volume both maintained roughly 30% year-over-year growth. Full-year Flash Sale users exceeded 500 million, and daily non-food orders surpassed 18 million. "Everything delivered in 30 minutes" is becoming Meituan's second growth engine alongside food delivery.

**In-store, hotel & travel remains the profit anchor.** Q4 revenue was approximately RMB 16.2 billion (+11% YoY), with an operating margin of 26%. GTV grew 15% year-over-year. Even as Douyin's local services GMV surpassed RMB 850 billion, in-store's profitability has not been undermined.

**Xiaoxiang Supermarket expanded to 39 cities.** The New Initiatives segment (covering grocery retail and overseas operations) generated full-year revenue of RMB 104 billion (+19%), with Q4 revenue of approximately RMB 27.3 billion (+19%). Xiaoxiang Supermarket is the core vehicle for grocery retail, and expansion has not slowed. A caveat: New Initiatives' Q4 operating loss ballooned to RMB 4.6 billion from Q3's RMB 1.3 billion — but this was mainly driven by the overseas Keeta business aggressively launching in the Middle East and Brazil during Q4, not by a deterioration in Xiaoxiang itself.

**Overseas Keeta turned unit economics positive in Hong Kong in Q4**, and has expanded to cover major Middle Eastern countries and Brazil. The short-term cash burn is intense (the primary driver behind New Initiatives' widening losses), but it demonstrates that Meituan's instant delivery model works internationally — a long-term option with real value.

**Both annual transacting users and purchase frequency hit all-time highs.** The subsidy war is painful, but users are staying and ordering more often.

Piecing it all together: food delivery is bleeding, but in-store and Flash Sale continue to grow healthily, Xiaoxiang is expanding into new cities, and the overseas business is being validated.

## The Key Variable for Valuation: How Far Will Operating Margins Recover?

There's little disagreement on the revenue side — from a base of RMB 364.9 billion, conservative estimates suggest the company can comfortably sustain 6–10% annual revenue growth. The real uncertainty lies in future operating margins. The 10.9% operating margin in 2024 was almost certainly a cyclical peak — that was an unusually benign competitive year, before the food delivery war erupted.

Under the government's policy stance against destructive competition, as the food delivery war gradually cools, Meituan could see operating margins turn positive again in 2026, with margins potentially recovering to around 8% by 2027.

A simplified DCF scenario analysis: assuming a 10% revenue CAGR over five years, operating margins gradually recovering to 8%, and a 10% discount rate, the per-share DCF value comes to roughly HKD 100. If operating margins only recover to 6%, the per-share DCF value drops to approximately HKD 80. The current share price sits roughly within this range, implying the market has largely priced in the expectation that the food delivery war will end and profits will return — but there remains disagreement on the extent of margin recovery.
