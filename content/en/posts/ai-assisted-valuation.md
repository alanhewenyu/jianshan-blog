## AI Will Not Replace Investment Judgment

Let's be clear about one thing first: AI will not replace the judgment of investors. The core of investment decisions — understanding business models, evaluating management, assessing competitive landscapes — these still require human insight.

But AI can dramatically improve the **efficiency** and **coverage** of investment analysis.

## Three Layers of AI Application in Investment Analysis

### Layer 1: Data Processing and Extraction

This is the most mature area of AI application:

- **Financial data extraction**: Automatically extracting key financial metrics from PDF annual reports
- **News monitoring**: Real-time tracking of company-related news and announcements
- **Data standardization**: Unifying data from different sources and formats

The value of this layer lies in saving vast amounts of repetitive work, allowing analysts to spend time on more valuable thinking.

### Layer 2: Analysis Assistance

AI can help us form preliminary judgments more quickly:

- **Financial summaries**: Rapidly generating key takeaways from quarterly/annual reports
- **Peer comparison**: Automatically calculating and comparing key financial ratios
- **Trend identification**: Discovering changes in revenue structure, profit margins, capital expenditure, and other metrics

However, note that AI-generated analysis requires manual verification. It may miss critical qualitative information or be misled by changes in accounting treatments.

### Layer 3: Valuation Modeling

This is the most cutting-edge area, and one that requires the most caution:

- **DCF parameter estimation**: Providing reasonable ranges for growth rates, profit margins, and other parameters based on historical data and industry comparisons
- **Scenario analysis**: Quickly generating valuation results under multiple scenarios
- **Sensitivity analysis**: Automating the calculation of how changes in key assumptions affect valuation

Our [Valux DCF](https://valux-dcf.streamlit.app/) tool is a practical application in this direction — it combines AI-assisted parameter generation with a traditional DCF framework to help investors perform valuation analysis more efficiently.

## Principles for Using AI

1. **AI is a tool, not a decision-maker**: The final investment judgment must be made by humans
2. **Verification first**: Any AI-generated data and analysis needs cross-verification
3. **Understand limitations**: AI excels at processing structured data but still has limited understanding of "soft information" (management integrity, corporate culture, etc.)
4. **Maintain independent thinking**: Do not abandon your own judgment just because AI produces a number

## Looking Ahead

The application of AI in investment analysis is still in its early stages. As large language model capabilities improve and financial data becomes more structured, we can expect:

- More accurate financial statement interpretation
- More comprehensive risk factor identification
- More efficient cross-market comparative analysis

But no matter how technology evolves, the core logic of value investing remains unchanged: find good businesses, buy at good prices, hold for the long term. AI simply helps us execute this process better.
