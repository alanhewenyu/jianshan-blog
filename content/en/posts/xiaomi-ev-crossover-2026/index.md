Xiaomi's 2025 revenue hit RMB 457.3 billion (+25%), with adjusted net profit at RMB 39.2 billion (+44%) — both all-time highs. Stripping out fair value gains, operating margin came in at 6.3%, up half a point from 5.9% last year. Looks fine on an annual basis. But Q4 alone was just 2.8%, nearly halved from Q3's 5.6% and well below the 4.8% in Q4 2024. The culprit: smartphone gross margin plunging from 11.1% in Q3 to 8.3%.

The stock has dropped from a high of HKD 61 to HKD 32. What is the market worried about?

## Where Does the Profit Come From?

Smartphones: RMB 186.4 billion revenue (41% of total), 10.9% gross margin, contributing RMB 20.3 billion in gross profit. IoT and lifestyle products: RMB 123.2 billion (27%), 23.1% margin, RMB 28.5 billion. Internet services: RMB 37.4 billion (8%), 76.5% margin, RMB 28.6 billion. Smart EV and new initiatives: RMB 106.1 billion (23%), 24.3% margin, RMB 25.8 billion.

Internet services generates only one-fifth the revenue of smartphones but contributes nearly the same gross profit — RMB 37.4 billion in revenue yielding RMB 28.6 billion in gross profit. This is the real profit engine, powered by 754 million monthly active users and 1.08 billion connected IoT devices. Every phone or IoT device sold feeds this high-margin flywheel. The smartphone segment's 10.9% margin looks thin, but its strategic value lies not in its own profitability — it's the traffic funnel.

Combined, the group gross margin of 22.3% is decent. Operating margin gets compressed to 6% by expenses: R&D at RMB 33.1 billion (7.2% of revenue) plus sales and marketing at RMB 33.2 billion (7.3%) consume nearly 15 percentage points. This isn't an efficiency problem — it's a business model choice. R&D includes heavy upfront investment in EVs and AI; sales expenses reflect offline retail expansion and international growth.

## Memory Chips Are Eating Smartphone Profits

Smartphone gross margins by quarter in 2025: Q1 12.8%, Q2 11.8%, Q3 11.1%, Q4 8.3%. Steady decline, accelerating in Q4.

The main driver is memory chip inflation. DRAM contract prices started climbing in late 2024 and progressively hit smartphone BOM costs through 2025. The logic traces back to AI — explosive demand for HBM (High Bandwidth Memory) in data centers led Samsung and SK Hynix to redirect DRAM capacity toward HBM, squeezing supply for conventional mobile DRAM. NAND was similarly tight, with prior production cuts compounding AI server SSD demand, pushing cumulative price increases above 40%. Memory chips account for roughly 15-20% of smartphone BOM — price hikes directly erode margins.

Annual smartphone shipments reached 165 million units, maintaining a top-three global position for five consecutive years. The position is solid, but the global smartphone market is largely zero-sum. Premiumization is underway — 25% market share in China's RMB 4,000-5,000 segment — but full-year ASP of RMB 1,129, down 0.8% from 2024's RMB 1,138, suggests premium gains haven't fully offset mid-to-low-end drag.

Q4's 8.3% is a critical number. If memory prices peak in H2 2026, smartphone margins could recover to 10-12%. But if the upcycle drags on, this cash cow keeps bleeding. President Lu Weibing hinted on the earnings call that price increases aren't off the table, though market share preservation remains the priority — raising prices could relieve margin pressure but risks volume impact, especially in China's increasingly competitive market following Huawei's return.

## 411,000 Cars Delivered — How Much Did They Earn?

411,000 units × RMB 251,000 average price ≈ RMB 103.3 billion in revenue. Including related services of RMB 2.8 billion, segment revenue totaled RMB 106.1 billion. Gross margin improved from 18.5% in 2024 to 24.3% — scale economics materializing. Full-year operating profit just turned positive at RMB 0.9 billion, barely breakeven, but considering 2024's RMB 6.2 billion loss, a RMB 7.1 billion swing is significant. However, Q4 margin retreated from 25.5% in Q3 to 22.7%, and management flagged potential margin pressure in 2026.

Management has consistently under-promised on delivery guidance: 2024 target of 100,000, delivered 137,000; 2025 target of 300,000, delivered 411,000. The 2026 target is 550,000 units. The new-generation SU7 launched March 19 attracted 15,000 locked orders in 34 minutes and exceeded 30,000 within three days. But this year's competitive landscape is tougher — purchase tax subsidies halved, Tesla cutting prices, Li Auto and Huawei's AITO competing in the RMB 200-300K segment.

## What Operating Margin Is the Current Stock Price Implying?

Revenue growth is relatively predictable: EV scaling from 411,000 to 550,000 units alone drives 30%+ segment growth, and combined with natural IoT and internet services expansion, 10-15% group revenue growth in 2026 is a conservative assumption; CAGR of 10% over the next few years seems reasonable. Anchoring on revenue, a reverse DCF (WACC 11%, capital efficiency at historical levels) implies the current HKD 32 stock price is pricing in roughly 6% operating margin — essentially no improvement from current levels. The market is saying "margins won't get better."

How pessimistic is this? A simple floor calculation: if smartphone margins merely recover from 8.3% to 10% (well below 2024's 12.6%), other segments hold steady, EV delivers 550,000 units at 24% gross margin — group operating margin reaches roughly 6.6%. Factor in internet services' natural penetration as the device base grows and IoT margins' upward trend, and 7-8% becomes plausible.

The core debate comes down to this: **Is 6% operating margin a cyclical trough or a structural ceiling?** If memory chips eventually peak, EV scale economics keep delivering, and monetization of 1 billion IoT devices is just beginning — 6% is the trough. If smartphones remain a low-margin red ocean, EV profitability stalls as subsidies fade, and heavy R&D and sales spending is permanent rather than temporary — 6% is the ceiling.

Leading indicators to watch: monthly new SU7 deliveries in April-May (45,000 units is the bar), Q1 earnings smartphone margin (can it bounce from 8.3%), DRAM/NAND spot price trends, and YU7 order data after its H2 launch.
