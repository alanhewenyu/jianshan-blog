China Literature's 2025 annual report looks ugly at first glance: revenue of RMB 7.37 billion (-9.3%), IFRS net loss of RMB 776 million. But dig into the numbers, and the loss is almost entirely from an RMB 1.8 billion goodwill impairment on New Classics Media — a legacy from the RMB 15.5 billion acquisition back in 2018. Strip out the impairment, share-based compensation, and other non-recurring items, and adjusted operating profit comes in at RMB 735 million with a 10% margin, while adjusted net profit is RMB 860 million. More importantly, after two consecutive years of write-downs, the New Classics goodwill has been **fully wiped out**. That ticking time bomb is gone from 2026's P&L.

Running a three-scenario FCFF-based DCF, China Literature's valuation range falls roughly between HKD 25-58. Relative valuation (15-22x 2026E adjusted PE plus net cash add-back) points to HKD 29-37. Cross-referencing the two methods, the combined range sits at roughly HKD 32-40. The stock trades around HKD 26. What is the market pricing in? A reverse DCF suggests roughly 1% medium-term growth and an 8% operating margin — close to the bear case.

## Three Years of MAU Halving — How Should We Read It?

Monthly active users dropped from 286 million in 2022 to 138 million in 2025 — a 52% decline over three years. The number looks alarming, but the breakdown tells a different story: monthly paying users (MPU) barely moved, holding steady around 9 million; monthly ARPU climbed from RMB 29.5 to RMB 32.9; and the paying ratio rose from 3% to 6.5%.

Who left? Mainly free riders — light users who migrated to Tomato Novel (MAU 218 million) and QiMao, both free reading platforms. The core paying users remain sticky. At 9 million MPU times RMB 32.9 monthly ARPU, that's roughly RMB 3.56 billion per year. China Literature's revenue breaks into two segments: paid reading (RMB 4.05 billion, 55%) and IP operations (RMB 3.32 billion, 45%, covering film/TV, comic dramas, merchandise, etc.). The paid reading figure aligns well with the MPU-times-ARPU calculation.

The risk: the funnel is shrinking. MAU is the top-of-funnel for paying conversion, and a smaller pool constrains future MPU growth. If MPU starts declining (it dipped 1.1% in 2025), the paid reading business shifts from "stable cash cow" to "shrinking asset."

## What Does RMB 9.4 Billion in Net Cash Mean?

Net cash of RMB 9.436 billion accounts for 36.5% of market cap. Subtract that, and the market is valuing the core business at roughly RMB 16.4 billion. Against 2025 adjusted net profit of RMB 860 million, the ex-cash PE is about 19x. Using the 2026 consensus estimate of RMB 1.3-1.4 billion in adjusted net profit, that PE drops to around 12x.

The cash pile is also generating returns — short-term investments grew from RMB 1 billion in 2023 to RMB 6 billion in 2025, producing meaningful interest income. Management launched a RMB 1 billion buyback program in late 2025 but has yet to announce a dividend.

## New Growth Engines: Can AI Comic Dramas and IP Merchandise Move the Needle?

The most interesting developments in 2025 weren't in the "core business" but in several new lines:

- **AI comic dramas**: H2 revenue crossed RMB 100 million, with nearly 1,000 titles launched. This is a zero-to-one category — using AI to automatically convert web novels into comic/animated short dramas. Production costs are far below traditional film/TV, but the business model and margin profile remain unproven.
- **IP merchandise**: GMV doubled from roughly RMB 500 million in 2024 to over RMB 1.1 billion. The financials don't break out merchandise margins separately — it's lumped into the broader IP operations segment, so the actual profit contribution remains unclear.
- **Short dramas**: Over 120 titles produced, with roughly 60% turning a profit — a solid hit rate for the short drama industry.
- **Overseas WebNovel**: AI-powered translation drove 39% revenue growth, now accounting for one-third of platform revenue.

Together, these new businesses drove structural change within the IP operations segment in 2025 — even as reduced film/TV scheduling dragged segment revenue down 18.9% to RMB 3.32 billion, the incremental contributions from AI comic dramas, merchandise, and short dramas partially offset the decline. The issue is that sub-segment margins aren't separately disclosed, and the overall profitability of IP operations still hinges on the cadence of major film/TV releases. In the DCF base case, assuming AI comic dramas contribute RMB 300-400 million in 2026 revenue, merchandise GMV reaches RMB 1.6-1.8 billion, and film/TV scheduling normalizes, IP operations revenue could recover to around RMB 3.8 billion, pushing total revenue back to RMB 8 billion.

But if these new engines decelerate in 2026 and fail to generate meaningful profit, we're back to the bear case — total revenue of RMB 6.9 billion, adjusted net profit of RMB 800 million, worse than 2025.

## The Two Most Sensitive DCF Parameters

Running a three-scenario DCF (WACC 10-12%, RONIC = WACC), valuation is most sensitive to two inputs:

1. **Operating margin**: Base case assumes 12%, bear case 9%, bull case 16%. Each percentage point shift moves the valuation by 5-7%. China Literature's adjusted operating margin declined from 12.1% in 2024 to 10.0% in 2025. Whether it stabilizes depends on whether the high-margin paid reading business holds up, and whether new businesses deliver profitability alongside growth.

2. **Revenue growth rate**: The base case assumes revenue rebounds to +9% in 2026 with a 6% medium-term growth rate thereafter; the bear case assumes continued contraction; the bull case assumes new business explosion driving 20%+ growth. Revenue has oscillated between RMB 7.0-8.1 billion over the past four years with no clear directional trend — whether this represents a "bottoming out" or "structural stagnation" is the single biggest point of debate in the current valuation.

## Where's the Disagreement?

The core debate around China Literature boils down to one question: **Is this a shrinking paid reading platform, or a content factory with improving IP monetization efficiency?**

If the former, halving MAU and declining revenue is the dominant narrative. Valuation should anchor on paid reading's steady-state profits — roughly RMB 3.5-4.0 billion in revenue, 10% operating margin, plus net cash, pointing to HKD 25-29.

If the latter, AI comic dramas, merchandise, short dramas, and overseas translation represent multiple growth curves where the same IP can be monetized across more touchpoints. Revenue could recover to RMB 8-10 billion with margin expansion potential, pointing to HKD 39-58.

**Signals worth tracking:**
- Whether MPU drops below 8.5 million in H1 2026 (paid ecosystem health)
- Whether AI comic drama quarterly revenue sustains sequential growth (new engine validation)
- Whether 2026 revenue returns to positive year-over-year growth (inflection confirmation)
- Buyback execution pace and potential dividend initiation (management confidence)
