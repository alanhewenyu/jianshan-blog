## What Made DCF Valuation So Difficult in the Past?

If you've ever tried calculating DCF yourself, you've surely encountered two core challenges.

**First, where does the data come from?** DCF requires two types of data: historical financials and valuation assumptions (such as future revenue growth rates and WACC). The simplest approach is reading annual reports directly, but pulling multi-year data and observing trends manually is extremely tedious. Some stock platforms can export historical statements, but the raw data still needs further processing for valuation purposes.

A more advanced approach is connecting to financial data APIs — Wind is common in China, Bloomberg is the gold standard globally. But these services are expensive and have high barriers for retail investors. Even with professional data sources, there's often a one-to-two-day delay — earnings announcements may already be out while the API still shows stale data.

**Second, how do you build the model?** The most common approach is Excel, but the manual work is enormous, error-prone, and inefficient. You could build a programmatic DCF model, but even then, the subjective assumptions — revenue growth rates, operating margins, discount rates — still require human judgment.
