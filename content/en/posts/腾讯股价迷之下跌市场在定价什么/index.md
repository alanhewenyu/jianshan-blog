Tencent Holdings' stock price has fallen more than 10% over the past two weeks, closing at HK$535.5 on February 12, hitting a recent low.

As for the reasons behind the decline, initial market chatter pointed to concerns about a potential VAT rate hike — if the three major telecom operators saw their rates increase, companies like Tencent might follow. Then came the controversy around Yuanbao sending red packets within WeChat, an apparent internal conflict. Most recently, the narrative shifted to Tencent falling behind in AI.

These stories are either speculative or impossible to verify. Granted, the AI lag may seem apparent — Tencent's Yuanbao primarily uses DeepSeek, lacking a proprietary model that ranks as highly as Alibaba's Qwen, while ByteDance's Seedance 2.0 video generation capabilities are truly impressive.

But does not building your own wheels, or not having the most cutting-edge proprietary model, necessarily hurt the business? I don't think so. Apple doesn't build its own AI foundation model, yet its moat remains intact. No matter how you look at it, Tencent is a major AI beneficiary — historical data already shows AI's contribution to its advertising efficiency, gaming is clearly the next opportunity, and WeChat's unique ecosystem has become a fast-growing search entry point (including AI search).

Let's set aside the noise and return to valuation — based on Tencent's historical financials, what is the company actually worth?

## The Misleading P/E Ratio

From a relative valuation perspective, Tencent's current trailing P/E is around 20x. Given its current sub-10% revenue growth rate, this doesn't look obviously undervalued.

However, the P/E ratio is quite misleading when applied to Tencent. Beyond its three core businesses — gaming, advertising, and fintech — Tencent held approximately RMB 1.1 trillion in listed and unlisted equity investments as of its September 2025 quarterly report. This portfolio contributes only modest investment income and fair value changes to the income statement.

![Tencent Investment Portfolio and Market Cap Breakdown](img_1.png)

Tencent's current market cap is approximately HK$4.9 trillion (about RMB 4.3 trillion). Subtracting the RMB 1.1 trillion investment portfolio, the market cap attributable to core operations is only about RMB 3.2 trillion — implying a "real" P/E of roughly 15x. That's quite attractive.

(Note: This doesn't even account for net cash — Tencent's cash minus interest-bearing debt leaves approximately RMB 100 billion in net cash.)

## DCF Valuation

Let's look at the DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) valuation. Here's Tencent's revenue growth and operating margin over recent years:

| Year | Revenue (RMB bn) | YoY Growth | Operating Profit (RMB bn) | Operating Margin |
|------|------------------|------------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| FY2020 | 482.1 | — | 126.2 | 26.2% |
| FY2021 | 560.1 | +16.2% | 124.7 | 22.3% |
| FY2022 | 554.6 | -1.0% | 110.8 | 20.0% |
| FY2023 | 609.0 | +9.8% | 160.1 | 26.3% |
| FY2024 | 660.3 | +8.4% | 208.1 | 31.5% |
| TTM 2025 | 729.8 | +10.5% | 232.7 | 31.9% |

*Source: Tencent annual reports and quarterly earnings. TTM = 2025 Q1-Q3 + 2024 Q4.*

After revenue stagnation in 2022, Tencent resumed growth in 2023, maintaining 8-10% annual revenue growth. Meanwhile, operating margins have steadily improved from 20% in 2022 to approximately 32% on a TTM basis — the primary driver of stock price appreciation over the past two years.

The margin improvement wasn't solely from cost-cutting. While 2022 was indeed the starting point — Tencent aggressively trimmed non-core businesses and headcount — the more critical factor has been the revenue mix shift: Video Account advertising, Mini Programs, and Mini Games (all high-margin) have grown their share, while AI-driven precision targeting has lifted advertising gross margins from 42% in 2022 to above 50%.

Based on the following DCF assumptions:

- 5-year revenue CAGR of approximately **8%**;
- Target operating margin of **36%**, driven by continued AI efficiency gains and higher-margin revenue mix;
- Reinvestment of approximately RMB 20 billion annually;
- WACC of approximately 8.5%, terminal growth rate of 2.5%.

Under these assumptions, Tencent's intrinsic value is approximately **~HK$700/share** — which happens to match Tencent's stock code (0700.HK).

## Reverse Engineering: What Does the Current Stock Price Imply?

Let's flip the question: instead of debating what Tencent should be worth, what revenue growth and operating margin does the current stock price actually imply?

Here's a sensitivity analysis of stock price relative to these two variables (HK$/share):

| Growth ＼ Op. Margin | 28% | 30% | 32% | 34% | 36% | 38% |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| 0% | 382 | 412 | 443 | 473 | 503 | 534 |
| 2% | 426 | 457 | 487 | 518 | 549 | 580 |
| 4% | 473 | 504 | 535 | 567 | 598 | 629 |
| 6% | 523 | 555 | 587 | 618 | 650 | 681 |
| 8% | 577 | 609 | 641 | 673 | 705 | 737 |
| 10% | 634 | 666 | 699 | 731 | 763 | 796 |

Key takeaways:

- Holding operating margin constant at ~32%, the current HK$535 stock price implies only **~4%** annual revenue growth over the next 5 years — roughly half of Tencent's actual current growth rate.
- Holding revenue growth constant at 8%, the current price implies a future operating margin of only **~26%** — 6 percentage points below current levels, and even below the 5-year average.

By either measure, the pessimism embedded in the current stock price significantly diverges from Tencent's actual operating performance.

## Summary

Short-term stock movements are often emotion-driven, but long-term prices inevitably revert to fundamentals. The market's pricing of Tencent appears to embed considerable pessimism. Whether this pessimism is justified is left to each reader's own judgment.
